Background Research & Problem Definition

The data, context, and real-world risks that define the problem NexLock One Touch is solving.

Background

Core Issue

Young children are naturally curious, and cabinets are one of the easiest places in the home for them to access harmful items. When medicines, cleaning products, detergents, or sharp utensils are stored in low or unlocked cabinets, a routine household space can quickly become dangerous. The core challenge is preventing access to these items while keeping cabinets easy for adults to use.

Root Causes

  • Dangerous products are often stored in low kitchen and bathroom cabinets.
  • Child-resistant packaging slows access but is not fully childproof.
  • Many cabinet locks are inconvenient, bulky, or frustrating to use consistently.
  • Parents may rely on supervision alone even though access can happen in seconds.
⚡

Design Opportunity

NexLock One Touch targets a simple goal: make cabinet safety automatic, compact, and reliable for medicines, cleaners, detergents, and other dangerous household items. A better cabinet lock should be easy for adults to use, hard for children to bypass, and simple enough to install in real homes.

Impact on Families

  • Emergency visits and poison control calls create real stress for families.
  • Many incidents involve everyday household items such as medicines, cleaners, detergents, and sharp kitchen tools.
  • Unsafe access can happen in seconds when cabinets are left low, unlocked, or inconsistently secured.
  • Families need cabinet safety systems that are easy enough to use every day, not just occasionally.
