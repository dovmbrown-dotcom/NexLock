Abstract

This project focuses on a modern cabinet-locking solution designed to prevent young children from accessing medicines, cleaning products, sharp utensils, and other dangerous household items. NexLock One Touch is built to make cabinet safety simpler, faster, and more reliable for families.

User Research Insights

Parents need cabinet safety that is simple enough to use every day. National safety guidance consistently shows that many dangerous items remain stored in low or unlocked cabinets, while poisoning prevention campaigns continue to emphasize that medicines and supplements left within reach send thousands of young children to emergency rooms every year.

Ideating the Solution

After identifying key safety gaps in existing cabinet-locking products, we developed and evaluated three potential design solutions.

Potential Solution #1

Mechanical Rack & Pinion Lock

A motorized rack-and-pinion system that uses claw-shaped hooks to secure cabinet doors.

How It Works

  • Gear rolls along an internal rack system
  • Hooks extend to lock the cabinet shut
  • Internal motor controls the mechanism
  • Buzzer alerts if the cabinet remains open

Strengths

  • Strong mechanical hold
  • Durable components
  • Clear locking mechanism

Limitations

  • Bulky internal system
  • More complex installation
  • Higher production cost

Potential Solution #2

Electromagnet Mount System

Two compact internal electromagnet mounts automatically secure cabinet doors when closed, helping prevent children from accessing medicines, cleaning products, and other dangerous household items stored inside cabinets.

How It Works

  • Magnets lock automatically when the cabinet shuts
  • Studs align to increase stability
  • External mounted button releases the magnet
  • Buzzer alerts if the cabinet stays open

Strengths

  • Compact and hidden
  • Simple installation
  • Lower cost components
  • Automatically locks
  • One-touch unlock

Limitations

  • Requires a power source
  • Must ensure magnet strength is reliable

Potential Solution #3

Biometric Deadbolt System

A fingerprint-activated deadbolt system powered by a motor.

How It Works

  • Fingerprint scan verifies the user
  • Motor rotates the deadbolt
  • Door unlocks if authenticated

Strengths

  • Personalized access
  • High-tech appeal
  • Secure authentication

Limitations

  • Expensive components
  • Over-engineered for cabinets
  • More complicated installation
